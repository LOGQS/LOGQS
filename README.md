# About me

Hi, I'm a mechanical engineering student who likes learning things and building practical tools, mostly for myself and the people around me.

## Background

Recently I've mostly worked on machine learning and agentic systems, though I've still had a lot of minor projects, from computational neuroscience to learning different coding stacks and languages. I got started through YouTube videos on reinforcement learning that were mostly for entertainment. I got interested and started working through the Hugging Face RL course. Around the same time I had a small idea for improving mechanical designs under selection pressure, using PPO and NEAT for evolving joints and shapes. ChatGPT came out a month or two into that, and it led me much deeper into ML, where I mostly stayed. Since then I've worked with a lot of languages, libraries, and tools, spending most of my time tinkering, reading, doing research, implementing existing architectures, improving on them, and trying to build my own.

## Notes on my repositories

A few honest notes: I'm not a tidy GitHub user, so some repos look rough or sit unfinished, and more than 80% of my work never makes it up here in a clean state, even though I keep it indexable in my personal setup. I try to add more when I can, especially with the pressure to market myself, find opportunities, and work with others. Feedback and contributions are always welcome and appreciated.

## Tooling

I use LLMs as helpers, but I try to own the code, meaning I know what's written and why. When time is tight, it's a trade-off I make. Some docs, commit messages, styling, or support files may be AI-assisted too; when they are, I tend to leave the traces in, like the over-commenting some models produce or an "ai-generated" note in the commit.
